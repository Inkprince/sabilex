ADMINISTRATION OF CRIMINAL JUSTICE ACT 2015 (ACJA) — ARREST AND DETENTION RIGHTS

Part 3 – Arrest. Section 4. Power of police to arrest without warrant. (1) A police officer may arrest a person without an order from a Magistrate and without a warrant— (a) who commits a cognisable offence in the presence or within the view of a police officer; (b) against whom a reasonable complaint has been made or credible information received or reasonable suspicion exists of his having been so concerned in the commission of a cognisable offence; (c) in whose possession anything is found which may reasonably be suspected to be stolen property. (2) A police officer shall not arrest a person on mere suspicion without any reasonable grounds or evidence of having committed an offence.

Section 6. Rights of an arrested person. (1) An arrested person shall be informed of the reason for his arrest in a language he understands. (2) An arrested person shall not be subjected to torture, inhuman or degrading treatment. (3) An arrested person shall be accorded humane treatment, having regard to his health and personal integrity. (4) An arrested person shall have the right to remain silent or avoid answering any question until after consultation with a legal practitioner of his own choice.

Section 8. Right to counsel. (1) Any person who is arrested or detained shall have the right to have a legal practitioner of his own choice immediately upon arrest. (2) Where the arrested person desires to retain a legal practitioner and requires assistance in contacting one, the police officer making the arrest shall provide the arrested person with reasonable assistance. (3) An arrested person who cannot afford a legal practitioner shall be entitled to legal aid, where available.

Section 9. Prohibition of detention in unauthorized places. No person shall be detained in any place other than a police station or prison. Any person detained in an unauthorized place shall be released forthwith upon application for a writ of habeas corpus.

Section 10. Period of detention without charge. (1) A suspect arrested in respect of an offence shall be charged to court within: (a) 24 hours of arrest for offences punishable with less than three years imprisonment; (b) 48 hours of arrest for all other offences; unless an order for further remand is obtained from a court. (2) If a suspect is not charged within the period specified, he shall be released on bail on such terms as the police officer in charge deems fit.

Section 14. Search of arrested person. Where a person is arrested by a police officer, the officer may search the arrested person, but the officer shall take special care not to expose the private person of the arrested individual and his belongings shall be carefully preserved.

Section 16. Bail. (1) A police officer of or above the rank of sergeant may grant bail to any person arrested for any non-capital offence. (2) Conditions of bail shall be reasonable and shall not be excessive. (3) Refusal to grant bail where it is appropriate is unlawful.

Part 4 – Charge and Arraignment. Section 27. Right to speedy trial. Every suspect is entitled to be charged and tried within a reasonable time. A court shall ensure the expeditious trial of all criminal proceedings before it.

POLICE ACT 2020 — POLICE CONDUCT

Section 24. Prohibition of torture. No officer of the Nigerian Police Force shall— (a) torture, coerce or use any form of violence against any person in custody; (b) use abusive, insulting or degrading language toward any person; (c) make any false entry in a case diary or station diary; (d) take or accept any bribe or gratification.

Section 25. Duty to assist. (1) It is the duty of every police officer to— (a) protect life and property; (b) preserve law and order; (c) detect and prevent crimes; (d) enforce all laws and regulations with which they are charged. (2) A police officer shall respond promptly to distress calls and requests for assistance.

PRACTICAL RIGHTS SUMMARY — What you can do when arrested:
1. You have the right to remain silent until you speak with a lawyer.
2. You must be told why you are being arrested, in a language you understand.
3. You must be taken before a court within 24–48 hours.
4. You cannot be tortured, beaten, or degraded.
5. You can apply for bail for non-capital offences.
6. You can apply to a High Court for a writ of habeas corpus if unlawfully detained.
7. If you are unlawfully detained, you are entitled to compensation and a public apology (Constitution Section 35(6)).
