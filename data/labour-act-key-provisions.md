LABOUR ACT — KEY PROVISIONS ON EMPLOYMENT RIGHTS

Part I – Contracts of Employment. Section 1. Application of Part I. (1) This Part applies to workers who have entered into or work under a contract with an employer whether the contract is for manual labour, clerical work or otherwise, expressed or implied, oral or written, and whether it is a contract of service or apprenticeship. Section 3. Engagement of workers. An employer who engages a worker under a contract not already in writing shall, within three months of the beginning of the contract, provide the worker with a written statement specifying— (a) the name of the employer; (b) the name and address of the worker and the place and date of his engagement; (c) the nature of the employment; (d) if the contract is for a fixed term, the date when the contract expires; (e) the appropriate period of notice to be given by the party wishing to terminate the contract; and (f) the rates of wages and method of calculating wages, the manner and periodicity of payment of wages, and any terms and conditions relating to hours of work, holidays, and holiday pay.

Section 7. Minimum notice. (1) Where, by agreement, the period of notice required to terminate a contract of employment is less than: (a) one week in the case of a contract for a period of not less than three months but less than two years; (b) two weeks in the case of a contract for two years but less than five years; (c) one month in the case of a contract for five years or more — the minimum period of notice required by this Act shall apply. (2) Either party may waive his right to notice on any occasion or accept payment in lieu of notice.

Section 9. Contracts not to be inconsistent with Act. Any contract of employment, whether made before or after the commencement of this Act, that contains a term or condition that is less favorable to the employee than any term or condition made applicable to that employee by this Act shall be void to the extent of that inconsistency.

Section 11. Wrongful dismissal. A worker who considers that his employment has been wrongfully terminated by his employer may make a complaint to a Labour Officer.

Part II – Protection of Wages. Section 13. Wages to be paid in legal tender. (1) Every employer shall pay wages due to each worker under their contract of employment. Wages shall be paid directly to the worker in the legal tender of Nigeria (Naira). (2) Deductions from wages are permitted only in cases authorised by law or by the written agreement of the worker.

Section 15. Frequency of wage payment. Wages shall be paid at intervals not exceeding one month; provided that wages payable daily shall be paid at the end of each working day, and wages payable weekly shall be paid at the end of each working week.

Section 19. Unlawful deductions. An employer shall not make any deduction from wages or accept any payment from a worker for or in connection with the obtaining or continuance of employment of that worker except as permitted by law.

Part III – Termination and Dismissal. Section 20. Grounds for summary dismissal. An employer may summarily dismiss a worker only if the worker: (a) commits misconduct of such a degree as to justify instant dismissal; (b) commits theft, fraud or dishonesty; (c) willfully disobeys a lawful and reasonable order. Mere allegations of misconduct are not sufficient grounds for summary dismissal — due process must be followed.

Section 23. Certificate of service. A worker whose employment has terminated shall, upon request, be given a certificate showing the period of his service, the work done, and any other matters that may be prescribed.

Part IV – Special Provisions Relating to Young Persons. Section 58. Employment of children. No child who has not attained the age of twelve years shall be employed or work in any capacity except where he is employed by a member of his family in light work of an agricultural, horticultural or domestic character.

Part V – Forced Labour. Section 73. Prohibition of forced labour. (1) No person shall be required to perform forced labour. (2) "Forced labour" means any labour or service exacted from any person under the menace of any penalty, being labour or service for which the said person has not voluntarily offered himself.
